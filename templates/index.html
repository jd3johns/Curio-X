Background

The What

We are trying to help scientific researchers sort and collect data for things that are hard for computers to do, but easy for people to do. At this stage, Curios is simply put, an image sorting and classification game. We incorporate gamification and crowd-sourcing to achieve our goals.

The Why

Image recognition software is at an evolving but primitive state. The computing process and time required to sort and categorize images through software will take much longer than it would take people to do. A famous example would be the how Foldit users produced an accurate 3D model of the enzyme in just ten days, while the problem of how to configure the structure of the enzyme had been an unaccomplished goal of scientists for 15 years.

The How

We collect the data you provide, aggregate it, and put it through an algorithm to filter out inaccuracies. We will then visualize the data to easily determine trends and patterns. We are also tuning the engagement of the project to make this more fun and awesome.

The People

We are 4 Systems Design Engineering students at the University of Waterloo: Charles Wu, Jonathan Johnston, Robert Bon, Kenneth Chan. We are supervised by Professor Edith Law, and are doing this project as a part of our Fourth Year Design Project for Systems Design Engineering.
